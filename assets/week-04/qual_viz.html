Post-school and degree holders show almost identical full licence rates (~84%). This suggests licensing is driven more by occupational need than academic level — vocational pathways often require driving as much as graduate roles do. Notably, those with no qualifications (70.1%) outperform those with school qualifications only (66.7%), likely because they are more concentrated in trades and manual work where driving is essential.

The 16-18 no-qualification group has a near-zero full licence rate (0.9%) — but this is mostly an age effect, not a qualification effect. Almost no one that age has had the chance to earn any qualification yet. The qualification gap only stabilises meaningfully from age 25 onwards. The degree line starts null at 16-18 for the same reason: too few data points to report.
